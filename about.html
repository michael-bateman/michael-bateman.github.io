About


Biography

For those who do not know me, my name is Michael Bateman. Since you’ve probably been searching around on this website for more information about me, you probably have a little knowledge of who I am. I live in Toronto and I go to school at Upper Canada College. I am a trombone player, former hockey player and rower. I also love all things to do with computers and computer science is a favourite. My original sport was hockey, but then in 2015 I had a horrible injury which caused me to be injured for a while and be unable to play. After that, I decided that I would give rowing a try. So for the past 2 years I’ve been rowing at Upper Canada College. If I am not rowing, and not doing school work, you can often find me trying a computer science project out.


Experience

I am an experienced software beta tester, blogger and food tester. I test prerelease software from Apple, which includes it’s well known software such as iOS 10 and macOS Sierra. I have also tested iOS 8 and 9, and Yosemite and El Capitan. I run my own blog, which you can visit here. It’s a lot of fun to blog. I also love computer science, and to learn more about that, you should probably visit my computer science page.


Acheivements

I have won some very interesting things and most of them when I played hockey. When I played hockey with the Leaside Flames AA team, I was one of the two MVP's. My rowing so far, has been pretty uneventful. The most success that I have has so far came at The 143th Royal Canadian Henley Regatta, finishing 4th. Other notable finishes include the Head of the Genesee Regatta where my flyweight 4+ came 3rd. I won many music awards as a band. I won two Ontario Band Association gold medals in grade 7, and a OBA gold medal in grade 8. I have also won a gold medal in the grade 8, Upper Canada College Concert Band.


Eduaction

I am working towards earning my Ontario Secondary School Diploma and my IB Diploma, once I graduate from my high school, Upper Canada College in 2020. I am also working to earn my Duke of Ed Bronze award in six months. In grade 7 at Upper Canada College, I was on the honour roll which means that a student achieves over 80% average.
